Research Description
Several evidence-based interventions (EBIs) for depression have been adapted and demonstrated efficacious in low-resource settings but few of these innovations have been brought to scale within ‘real world’ health systems. As a leading cause of death in low-income settings that is driven, in part, by high comorbidity with Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), tuberculosis (TB) represents a strategic point-of-entry for integrating MDD treatment in primary care systems in low resource settings. The overall aims of this proposal are

  • To gain expertise in scale-up focused dissemination and implementation science (D&I) and social network analysis (SNA)
  • To develop a targeted scale-up strategy for integrating MDD-EBI treatment into primary care using tuberculosis (TB) services in Brazil as a novel point-of-entry.
    • Responsibilities
  • Wrangling data, visualization, and breaking up the data into various levels for a thorough understanding of the data
  • Performing assumption check, statistical examination such as a paired t-test, and network model construction to measure the diffusion of innovations
